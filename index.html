Multi Snap

SUMMARY

Multi Snap is a feature that allows users to record, edit and send up to six Snaps simultaneously. Previously the app would limit video recording to one 10 second Snap. With Multi Snap, you can keep recording up to a minute to capture the whole moment.

This was one of the most impactful projects that the company released in 2017. It resulted in a 14% increase in Story posts across the platform, and more than half of all videos on Snapchat are now Multi Snaps.


MOTIVATION

When Snapchat first launched, it only supported photos that disappeared after up to ten seconds. When we released videos, we kept the ten second limit because we wanted to maintain the notion that viewing a Snap was super quick. Since users open Snapchat several dozen times a day on average, we wanted to be sure that they could still easily jump in and out of the app.

With that said, we found that half of all videos on Snapchat were ten seconds long, resulting in content that was being abruptly cut off. It was clear that users wanted to take longer videos. We also noted an emergent behavior among influencers who were beginning to use the camera in a different way: rather than taking quick selfies, they were broadcasting long-form Stories by taking sequences of back-to-back Snaps. With this use-case, the ten second limit was becoming a burden.


PROTOTYPING

Evan asked me to prototype several solutions to expanding the ten second video limit, including a single longer Snap and a timeline UI. We went with the Multi Snap approach - Snaps remain ten seconds long, and videos that are longer are split into multiple Snaps. This respects the notion that a Snap is a quick bite of content. We didn’t want to turn the experience of watching Snaps into watching YouTube videos. Furthermore, since the average Snap viewing duration is well under one second, a receiving user can tap multiple times to ‘scrub’ through a Multi Snap video sequence, as opposed to tapping once to skip the whole video.

Lastly, we designed the UI of editing multiple Snaps at once to be flexible. The feature is extensible - it could plausibly support sources of content other than the immediate Snap camera. This might give the product the capacity to be a generalized Story editor.


RECORDING A MULTI SNAP

I can press and hold the record button to begin recording a Snap. Once the video duration reaches ten seconds, I begin to seamlessly record a consecutive Snap.

The first time a user records a video that reaches 8s long, we pop a tooltip that prompts them to keep holding to create a Multi Snap.

Each time a ten second interval has completed recording, a thumbnail slides on the screen from the left to indicate that a new Snap was created.


EDITING A MULTI SNAP

Once I finish recording, I enter the Preview Screen where I can edit my Snaps.

If I tap a thumbnail, the video seeks to that thumbnail.

I can hide all the UI by swiping.

I can press and hold a Snap to delete it in the trash can.

If I draw on the screen, then it autohides.

When I perform an edit - filter, text, etc - it automatically updates the thumbnails accordingly.

When I press the Send button, the sequence is sent as a series of individual Snaps in chronological order.


Pull-to-Refresh

SUMMARY

Pull to Refresh was an animation project. The ghost hides behind the card, and peeks out as you pull down. This is a reference to Picaboo. When you pull down enough, the ghost winks. When you release, we do a rainbow effect.


ENGINEERING

I prototyped and developed the animation in OpenGL for performance. We split the screen in half. One shader is for the wave, the other is for the rainbow. The wave is calculated using trignometry and replicating the After Effects warp effect. When we let go, we use realistic spring physics from the POP library to make the wave peak amplitude based on the scroll offset. When we let go, we animate the gradient layer horizontally against a base layer with a blend mode to achieve the ripple effect.


EASTER EGG

If you pull far enough, the ghost gets scared and the screen shakes. As you pull further down, the haptic rate increases. When you release, the speed of the launch and the haptic on release is intensified.


Attachments

SUMMARY

Attachments is a creative tool that allows users to search for a web URL and attach it to a Snap. After taking a Snap, I can select the Paperclip tool. I can then search for a URL or paste one from my clipboard. Once I attach the webpage, a friend can view it by swiping up on the Snap once they open it.


IMPACT

Attachments allowed us to help brands monetize. We have features like the Snapchat Store, where users can swipe up to purchase things. This was crucial in that it allowed Snapchat ads to result in an in-app conversion.

It was a form of expression. It allowed users to send YouTube videos, for videos longer than ten seconds.

It also created an organic ecosystem where third party developers would create experiences on the Attachments platform. One example is Polly, Sarahah, and Story Replies. Part of my responsibilities post-launch was reaching out to these developers and understanding how we can support them.


MOTIVATION

Wanted to use Attachments as a way to segue into Context.

Context is a project that turns a Snap from a photo or a video, into a container. You can swipe up to learn more about the world.

You could imagine how this system is flexible so that you can attach things other than web pages.

We started with explicit user-input. Then we launched Context, which attempts to automatically gather additional info about the Snap and present it to you alongside.


ADDING AN ATTACHMENT

Once I open the Paperclip tool, I can search for a URL, quickly reattach a link I previously attached, or automatically attach a URL from my clipboard.


PRIVACY + SECURITY

Safesearch is on. We ask for permission before using the clipboard. Spoofing issue - HTTPS icon.
